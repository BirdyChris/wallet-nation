Why it matters

Digital asset payments are irreversible by design. Protection should not require a custodian.

On-chain settlement has no chargeback or reversal mechanism. Once a transaction confirms, it is final — errors, disputes, and fraud cannot be undone. Wallet Nation closes that gap with a non-custodial conditional-release layer that protects both parties before settlement becomes final.

How it works

How protected payments work.

Wallet Nation uses Transfer Wallet™ technology to protect digital asset payments by conditionally transferring control — not custody of funds.

01

Transfer Wallet created

A Transfer Wallet is created for the transaction. Cryptographic control credentials are generated. No funds are deposited yet.

02

Parties receive encrypted control credentials

Both transacting parties receive their encrypted credentials. Control is shared — no single party can act alone, and Wallet Nation holds no custody.

03

Funds deposited

The sender deposits funds into the Transfer Wallet. They remain under the cryptographic control of both parties — not Wallet Nation.

04

Conditions satisfied → control released

When agreed conditions are met — delivery confirmed, milestone reached, window expired — control is released and settlement completes on-chain.

05

Dispute raised → arbitrator resolves

If a dispute arises, an arbitrator reviews evidence and co-signs a resolution. The arbitrator cannot unilaterally move funds — rules are encoded, not discretionary.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

What is Wallet Nation?
Wallet Nation is a non-custodial transaction protection platform that introduces conditional settlement for digital asset payments. Rather than holding customer funds, Wallet Nation protects the transaction by controlling the Transfer Wallet™ until agreed conditions have been satisfied.
How is Wallet Nation different from traditional escrow?
Traditional escrow providers hold customer funds until a transaction is completed. Wallet Nation does not hold customer funds. Instead, it conditionally controls the Transfer Wallet, allowing the transacting parties to remain in control while introducing protection before settlement becomes final.
Does Wallet Nation take custody of digital assets?
No. Wallet Nation is designed as a non-custodial platform. Customer assets remain under the control of the transacting parties throughout the transaction. Wallet Nation never operates as a custodian of customer funds.
What is Transfer Wallet™?
Transfer Wallet™ is Wallet Nation's patent-pending transaction mechanism. It enables digital asset transactions to be protected through conditional release rather than irreversible settlement, allowing agreed conditions to be met before control is transferred.
How does Wallet Nation resolve disputes?
If a dispute arises before settlement conditions have been satisfied, the Transfer Wallet remains protected while the parties seek resolution. Where required, an agreed independent arbitrator can review the evidence and assist in resolving the dispute.
Does Wallet Nation reverse blockchain transactions?
No. Wallet Nation does not reverse blockchain transactions. Instead, it introduces protection before settlement becomes final, reducing the need for reversals by preventing disputes before they occur.
Which digital assets are supported?
Wallet Nation is designed to be blockchain and asset agnostic. The architecture can support stablecoins, cryptocurrencies and other tokenised digital assets, depending on implementation by partners.
Who is Wallet Nation designed for?
Wallet Nation is designed for organisations that facilitate digital asset transactions, including: Payment platforms, Stablecoin providers, Crypto exchanges, Wallet providers, Enterprise payment platforms, Web3 marketplaces, OTC trading platforms.
Does Wallet Nation replace existing wallets or payment platforms?
No. Wallet Nation is designed to integrate alongside existing wallets, exchanges and payment infrastructure, adding an additional protection layer without requiring organisations to replace their current systems.
Why is non-custodial architecture important?
Keeping customers in control of their own assets reduces custodial complexity, improves security and preserves the self-sovereign principles of blockchain while still introducing trusted transaction protection. This aligns with the core architectural principles described in the Wallet Nation whitepaper.
Is Wallet Nation a consumer wallet?
No. Wallet Nation is infrastructure. It is licensed to payment providers, exchanges, wallet platforms and enterprises that want to introduce protected digital asset settlement into their existing products.
